‚ö†Ô∏è Disclosure Obligations & CPIA

Why This Matters When Using Copilot

When you use Copilot to search for information or conduct research as part of an investigation, you may be creating material that is subject to disclosure obligations under the Criminal Procedure and Investigations Act 1996 (CPIA).

Key Principle:

Investigators must pursue all reasonable lines of enquiry, whether these point towards or away from the suspect. This includes digital searches and AI-assisted research.

What is CPIA?

The Criminal Procedure and Investigations Act 1996 (CPIA) governs how investigators must:

  • Record - Document all material obtained during an investigation
  • Retain - Keep material that may be relevant to the case
  • Reveal - Disclose material to the prosecutor that might undermine the prosecution case or assist the defence

‚ö†Ô∏è CPIA - How It Applies to Copilot

What You DoWhat It CreatesDisclosure Implication
Search for informationSearch queries and resultsMay need to be recorded

Remember: The CPIA Code of Practice states that material may be relevant if it has some bearing on any offence under investigation, any person being investigated, or the surrounding circumstances of the case.

‚ö†Ô∏è CPIA - Best Practice

‚úÖ DO:

  • Keep a record of searches conducted using Copilot
  • Document what you asked and what results you received
  • Save relevant outputs that may assist or undermine the case
  • Consider whether results point towards OR away from the suspect
  • Include Copilot searches in your Investigation Management Document (IMD)
  • Treat AI-generated material the same as any other digital material

‚ùå DON'T:

  • Assume Copilot searches are not disclosable
  • Delete search history without considering disclosure obligations
  • Rely solely on AI summaries without verifying the information
  • Forget that both helpful AND unhelpful results may need disclosure
  • Use Copilot for investigation searches without proper record-keeping

‚ö†Ô∏è CPIA - Recording Your Searches

When using Copilot as part of an investigation, record:

  1. Date and time of the search
  2. The prompt/question you entered
  3. The response Copilot provided
  4. Your assessment of relevance to the investigation
  5. Any action taken as a result of the search

The Disclosure Test

Material must be disclosed if it:

  • Might reasonably be considered capable of undermining the case for the prosecution, OR
  • Might reasonably be considered capable of assisting the case for the accused

This applies to Copilot searches just as it applies to any other investigative material.

‚ö†Ô∏è CPIA - Key Questions to Ask Yourself

Before using Copilot for investigation-related work:

  1. Is this search part of a criminal investigation?
  2. Am I recording this search appropriately?
  3. Could the results (positive or negative) be relevant to the case?
  4. Have I considered lines of enquiry that point away from the suspect?
  5. Will this search need to be included in disclosure schedules?

Remember:

  • Copilot responses are AI-generated and must be verified
  • The disclosure officer is responsible for reviewing all relevant material
  • Failure to disclose relevant material can lead to cases collapsing
  • Always err on the side of caution - if in doubt, record and retain

Further Guidance

  • CPIA Code of Practice 2020 - Sets out investigator obligations
  • Attorney General's Guidelines on Disclosure 2022 - Includes Annex A on Digital Material
  • CPS Disclosure Manual - Detailed guidance on disclosure processes
  • Consult your Disclosure Officer if you are unsure
