‚ùå DON'Ts - Critical Rules to Follow

These are important restrictions to protect sensitive information and maintain professional standards:

üö´ Don't Look Up Information Outside Your Job Role

Rule: Only use Copilot for information relevant to your role and responsibilities.

Why: Maintains information barriers and prevents unauthorized access to sensitive data.

Example of what NOT to do: Searching for details about ongoing enforcement cases you're not involved with.

üîí Don't Input Sensitive or Confidential Information

Rule: Never input personal data, confidential firm information, market-sensitive data, or details of ongoing matters.

Why: Protects data privacy, market integrity, and confidential information.

Examples of what NOT to input:

  • Names of individuals under investigation
  • Confidential firm data from supervisory work
  • Market-sensitive information
  • Unpublished enforcement details

‚öñÔ∏è Don't Use Copilot Beyond Proportionate Purposes

Rule: Only use Copilot when it's proportionate to the task at hand.

Why: Ensures responsible use of AI tools and maintains professional judgment.

Ask yourself: Is using Copilot necessary and appropriate for this specific task?

üìä Don't Use Without Understanding Limitations

Rule: Always verify Copilot's outputs and apply your professional expertise.

Why: AI can make mistakes or provide incomplete information.

Remember: You are responsible for the accuracy of any work product.

üéØ Don't Rely Solely on Copilot for Critical Decisions

Rule: Use Copilot as a tool to assist, not replace, your professional judgment.

Why: Financial crime work requires human expertise, context, and decision-making.

Remember: Copilot enhances your work; it doesn't replace your expertise.

üéôÔ∏è Copilot Transcribe - Special Considerations

Copilot Transcribe (in Teams) has specific requirements you must follow:

Critical Requirements:

‚úÖ ALWAYS Inform Participants

Requirement: You MUST tell all meeting participants at the start that you are using Copilot Transcribe.

How to do this: "Just to let everyone know, I'm using Copilot Transcribe for this meeting to help with note-taking."

Why: Transparency, consent, and data protection compliance.

‚è∞ Transcripts Auto-Delete After 7 Days

Important: Copilot Transcribe automatically deletes transcripts after 7 days.

Action required: If you need to retain information for legitimate business purposes, save key points to appropriate systems BEFORE the 7-day deadline.

Don't: Save entire transcripts unnecessarily - only extract what's needed and proportionate.

‚öñÔ∏è Only Use When Proportionate

Ask yourself:

  • Is transcription necessary for this meeting?
  • Could simple manual notes suffice?
  • Does the benefit justify the data processing?

Examples of proportionate use: Complex technical meetings, multi-stakeholder discussions, meetings requiring detailed records.

Not proportionate: Routine catch-ups, informal discussions, meetings with minimal content.

‚ùå When NOT to Use Transcribe

Do not use Copilot Transcribe for meetings containing:

  • Highly confidential or market-sensitive information
  • Enforcement-related discussions with legal privilege
  • Personal or sensitive information about individuals
  • Information that should not be recorded or processed by AI

üîê Access During 7-Day Period

Remember: During those 7 days, consider who has access to the transcript in Teams.

Best practice: Use Transcribe only in meetings where all participants are authorized to see the content.

‚öñÔ∏è The Proportionality Principle

Always ask: "Is using Copilot proportionate to the task I need to accomplish?"

Understanding Proportionality:

‚úÖ Proportionate

Clear benefit, low risk

‚ö†Ô∏è Consider Carefully

Moderate benefit, some sensitivities

‚ùå Not Proportionate

Limited benefit, high risk

Questions to Ask Before Using Copilot:

  • Is this task within my role and responsibilities?
  • Does the information I'm using contain sensitive data?
  • Could I accomplish this effectively without Copilot?
  • What's the benefit vs. the risk of using AI for this task?
  • Am I using Copilot to enhance my work, or to replace professional judgment?
  • Would I be comfortable explaining my use of Copilot for this task?

When in Doubt, Don't

If you're unsure whether using Copilot is appropriate for a particular task, err on the side of caution and don't use it. Consult with your manager or compliance team if you need guidance.

üéØ Advanced Prompt Techniques

The 4 Keys to Good Prompts

A good prompt has four parts:

  1. üéØ Goal - What you want Copilot to do
  2. üìã Context - Background information
  3. üìÅ Source - Files or data to reference
  4. ‚úÖ Expectations - Format, tone, length

Example prompt:

"Draft an outline of a training manual about AML controls. Our audience is new starters who need to understand the basics. The tone should be professional but accessible."

Be Specific About Format

  • "Give me this as a table"
  • "Present this as bullet points"
  • "Write this in 100 words or less"
  • "Format this as a numbered list"

Set the Tone

  • "Make this sound professional"
  • "Write this in a friendly, casual tone"
  • "Keep this formal but approachable"
  • "Explain this as if talking to someone new to the topic"

Use Role-Based Prompts

  • "Act as a policy expert and..."
  • "You are a training specialist helping me..."
  • "As a communications expert, explain..."

Chain Your Prompts

Start broad, then refine:

  1. "Give me 10 ideas for training topics"
  2. "Expand on idea 3"
  3. "Now make it suitable for new starters"
  4. "Create an outline for this"

‚ö†Ô∏è Disclosure Obligations & CPIA

Why This Matters When Using Copilot

When you use Copilot to search for information or conduct research as part of an investigation, you may be creating material that is subject to disclosure obligations under the Criminal Procedure and Investigations Act 1996 (CPIA).

Key Principle: Investigators must pursue all reasonable lines of enquiry, whether these point towards or away from the suspect. This includes digital searches and AI-assisted research.

What is CPIA?

The Criminal Procedure and Investigations Act 1996 (CPIA) governs how investigators must:

  • Record - Document all material obtained during an investigation
  • Retain - Keep material that may be relevant to the case
  • Reveal - Disclose material to the prosecutor that might undermine the prosecution case or assist the defence

How Does This Apply to Copilot Searches?

What You Do What It Creates Disclosure Implication
Search for information Search queries and results May need to be recorded
Ask Copilot questions Prompts and responses May be disclosable material
Generate summaries AI-generated content Should be reviewed and retained if relevant
Research suspects or topics Digital records Must be logged if part of investigation

CPIA Code of Practice Requirements

The CPIA Code of Practice states that:

  • Material may be relevant if it has some bearing on any offence under investigation, any person being investigated, or the surrounding circumstances of the case
  • Where material is held digitally, investigators must decide which material it is reasonable to inquire into and in what manner
  • A record or log must be made of all digital material seized or imaged and retained as relevant
  • The methodology of examination must be described accurately and clearly

‚úÖ DO:

  • Keep a record of searches conducted using Copilot
  • Document what you asked and what results you received
  • Save relevant outputs that may assist or undermine the case
  • Consider whether results point towards OR away from the suspect
  • Include Copilot searches in your Investigation Management Document (IMD)
  • Treat AI-generated material the same as any other digital material

‚ùå DON'T:

  • Assume Copilot searches are not disclosable
  • Delete search history without considering disclosure obligations
  • Rely solely on AI summaries without verifying the information
  • Forget that both helpful AND unhelpful results may need disclosure
  • Use Copilot for investigation searches without proper record-keeping

Recording Your Copilot Searches

When using Copilot as part of an investigation, record:

  1. Date and time of the search
  2. The prompt/question you entered
  3. The response Copilot provided
  4. Your assessment of relevance to the investigation
  5. Any action taken as a result of the search

The Disclosure Test

Material must be disclosed if it:

  • Might reasonably be considered capable of undermining the case for the prosecution, OR
  • Might reasonably be considered capable of assisting the case for the accused

This applies to Copilot searches just as it applies to any other investigative material.

Key Questions to Ask Yourself

Before using Copilot for investigation-related work:

  1. Is this search part of a criminal investigation?
  2. Am I recording this search appropriately?
  3. Could the results (positive or negative) be relevant to the case?
  4. Have I considered lines of enquiry that point away from the suspect?
  5. Will this search need to be included in disclosure schedules?

Remember

  • Copilot responses are AI-generated and must be verified
  • The disclosure officer is responsible for reviewing all relevant material
  • Failure to disclose relevant material can lead to cases collapsing
  • Always err on the side of caution - if in doubt, record and retain

Further Guidance

  • CPIA Code of Practice 2020 - Sets out investigator obligations
  • Attorney General's Guidelines on Disclosure 2022 - Includes Annex A on Digital Material
  • CPS Disclosure Manual - Detailed guidance on disclosure processes
  • Consult your Disclosure Officer if you are unsure about obligations
