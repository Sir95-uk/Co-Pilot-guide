FCA - Financial Crime

Welcome to the Copilot Guidance

This interactive guide will help you understand how to use Copilot effectively and responsibly within the FCA, with a focus on Financial Crime use cases.

Key Objectives:

  • Understand Copilot's capabilities and limitations.
  • Learn effective prompting techniques.
  • Ensure compliance with FCA data security and privacy policies.
  • Explore specific Financial Crime scenarios.
Important Note: Copilot is a tool for *assistance*, not *automation*. All outputs must be reviewed and verified by a human, especially concerning sensitive data or regulatory advice.

Capabilities & Limitations

Copilot can:

  • Summarize long documents, emails, and reports.
  • Draft communications, reports, and code snippets.
  • Analyze data trends (if data is provided in context).
  • Suggest improvements to existing text/code.

Copilot cannot:

  • Make decisions or provide regulatory approval.
  • Access real-time, external internet data (in this configuration).
  • Guarantee 100% accuracy; it can hallucinate or misinterpret context.
Click to learn more about 'Hallucinations'

Hallucinations: This is when the AI generates convincing-sounding but factually incorrect information. Always cross-reference facts and figures.

Effective Prompting Techniques

The quality of Copilot's output depends on the quality of your input (the prompt).

✅ DO: Be Specific and Provide Context

Prompt: "Summarise the attached 'MLRO Report 2024.docx' for the head of department. Focus on the three highest-risk areas identified and potential regulatory impact. Keep it under 200 words."

❌ DON'T: Be Vague

Prompt: "Summarise this report."

DO: Use positive instructions and specify the desired format (e.g., "Use bullet points", "In the style of an FCA RDC notice").
DON'T: Assume Copilot knows FCA jargon or internal policies without explicitly telling it.

Data Security & Privacy

FCA systems are configured to protect data, but user responsibility is key.

NEVER input highly sensitive, non-public, or personal data (PII) into the Copilot chat interface if you are unsure of the security guidelines.

Proportionality Scale for Data Input:

Low Risk (Public data, general info)
Medium Risk (Internal, non-sensitive data)
High Risk (Sensitive PII, confidential investigations)

When in doubt, use general queries or anonymise your data before prompting.

Financial Crime Scenarios

Here are some ways Copilot can assist with specific tasks:

SAR Review Assistance

Prompt: "Analyze the attached Suspicious Activity Report (SAR). Extract all named entities, transactions dates, and mention the specific typologies (e.g., money laundering, terrorist financing) mentioned. Format as a bulleted list for a case manager."

Reminder: This helps organize data, but a human must assess the risk and determine next steps.

Policy Drafting

Prompt: "Draft a new internal policy section on cryptocurrency monitoring for the Financial Crime Guide. Ensure it aligns with current FCA guidance on cryptoassets and includes mandatory training requirements for front-line staff."

Training Material Creation

Prompt: "Create a short, interactive quiz with 5 multiple-choice questions based on the 'Market Abuse Regulation' document provided. Include correct answers and brief explanations for incorrect choices."

Key Takeaways & Checklist

Your Responsible Use Checklist:

  • ✅ Treat Copilot as an assistant, not a decision-maker.
  • ✅ Verify all facts, figures, and regulatory interpretations.
  • ✅ Be highly specific and provide clear context in your prompts.
  • ✅ Exercise extreme caution with sensitive PII and non-public data.
  • ✅ Be aware of AI hallucinations and potential biases.

During a Teams meeting, you can ask Copilot: "Summarize what I've missed in the last 10 minutes" or "List action items discussed so far."
